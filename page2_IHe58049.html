Imbursa Aquarium

Founded in 2014, and located in Mexico City, Mexico, Imbursa Aquarium is also considered the largest aquarium in Latin America. As a curious fact, the architecture of this aquarium is underground, which in turn has 5 levels, which counts on about 48 exhibitions and nearly 4,000 specimens of more than 350 species among crocodiles, seals, stingrays, sharks, octopuses, penguins, craps, jellyfish, and many more.
Within the numerous attractions of this aquarium is the physical interaction with certain harmless marine species for human safety, which are starfish, turtles, non-poisonous frog species, among many others. Of course, these interactions are supervised by expert animal keepers and trainers who are part of the aquarium team.

Oceanarium and Jellyfish maze

Another of the main attractions of the Inbursa aquarium are the oceanarium and the jellyfish maze. The oceanarium section focuses on the exhibition and care of different species of sharks, as well as rays, various species of fish and turtles, whereas the jellyfish section focusses on presenting huge number of different species of jellyfish and the beautiful colors they can reflect from their bodies. In both sections we can find both, hatchlings and grown specimens' families
